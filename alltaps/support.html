Wait, that’s it?

Yep. On purpose.

AllTaps is small, fast, and stays out of your way. It lives on your wrist — not on a dashboard you’ll never look at, not in a CRM of your shots, not in a feed asking how you felt about hole 7. We obsessed over what to leave out as much as what to put in.

What we deliberately left out (and why)

  • No GPS, yardage, or hole maps. Other apps do that beautifully. This isn’t that.
  • No swing analysis. We don’t have an opinion about your swing.
  • No subscriptions, no signup, no “premium” tier. The app you download is the app you get.
  • No leaderboards, no social feed, no friends list. Your league has a group text. Use that.
  • No notifications. You’ll never get a push from AllTaps telling you to “come back.”
  • No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking. We don’t know who you are, and we’d like to keep it that way.

What we obsessed over instead

  • One tap = one shot. That’s the whole interaction model.
  • Big buttons. Watch screens are tiny. Thumbs aren’t. We optimized for thumbs.
  • Save automatically, resume anywhere. Watch dies, sun gets in your eye, you lose track mid-round — AllTaps remembers.
  • Tiny app, tiny battery footprint. No background syncing, no pinging the cloud, no “always on” nonsense. Open it, count, close it.
  • Cute, sharable scorecards. If the app’s job is over in 30 seconds, the least we can do is leave you with something fun to text your friends.

If you want a Swiss Army knife, the App Store is full of them. We made a tee. ⛳

Frequently asked questions

How do I save my round?

AllTaps saves automatically after every shot. There’s also a Save & Exit button at the bottom of the round screen if you want to leave the app and come back later — your progress will be waiting on the home screen as “Resume Round.”

I tapped the counter too many times. Can I undo a shot?

Yes — tap the coral − button below the counter to remove the last shot from the current hole. You can also use the back arrow to revisit a previous hole and edit its score.

How do I share my scorecard?

After you finish a round, tap Share Scorecard on the scorecard screen. AllTaps renders a scorecard image and hands it to Apple’s system share sheet, where you can send it via Messages or Mail. Your Apple Watch needs to be paired and connected to your iPhone for the share sheet to complete the hand-off.

Where are my past rounds?

From the home screen, tap Previous Rounds. (The button only shows up after you’ve completed at least one round.) Tap any round in the list to see its scorecard again or re-share the image.

How do I delete a round or all my data?

For now, the simplest way is to uninstall AllTaps — every saved round is erased instantly. There’s nothing in the cloud to clean up because nothing was ever sent there.

Per-round delete is on our list for a future update.

Why doesn’t AllTaps have GPS / yardage / hole maps / par tracking?

By design. AllTaps is built to be the simplest possible counter — perfect for casual rounds, league nights, and anyone who finds full-featured golf apps to be overkill. If you want GPS and shot-by-shot analytics, lots of great paid apps do that. We do one thing: count.

Does AllTaps need an internet connection?

Nope. Once installed, AllTaps works completely offline. The only feature that touches the network is “Share Scorecard” — and that just hands off to Apple’s own share sheet (Messages or Mail).

Is my data private?

Yes. AllTaps collects nothing. See our Privacy Policy for the full details.

Will AllTaps come to iPhone or iPad?

Maybe. The watchOS app is the priority. If there’s enough demand for an iPhone companion, we’ll consider it.
