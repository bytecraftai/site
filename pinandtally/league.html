What it actually does

You open the app, tap Start a New Round, pick 9 or 18, dictate (or type) your course name if you want, and start tapping. One tap = one shot. There’s a giant pink button you can hit without looking. That’s really it.

When you finish a hole, swipe to the next. When you finish the round, the watch renders a little illustrated scorecard — your course, the date, every hole, the total — and you can text it to yourself or share it with a friend in one tap.

Things it does well

  • Big, satisfying counter button. Custom haptics. Soft pop sound.
  • 9 or 18 hole rounds. Save & exit mid-round, resume right where you left off.
  • Auto-resume — close the app, do something else, come back, you’re right back in your round.
  • A new greeting every time you open it. (You’ll see.)
  • Beautiful illustrated scorecards rendered fresh for each round.
  • Full round history on your wrist.

Things it intentionally doesn’t do

  • No GPS course-finder. No yardage. No swing analysis.
  • No accounts. No login. No email needed.
  • No notifications begging for your attention.
  • No ads. No upsells. No premium tier.

🔒 Private by design

Pin & Tally collects nothing. Every round stays on your wrist. No servers, no analytics, no third-party SDKs, no ads. I literally couldn’t see your scores if I tried — they don’t leave the device.

FAQ

How much does it cost?

Free at launch. No premium tier planned. If that ever changes I’ll be very loud about it.

Which watches is it for?

Apple Watch Series 7 or later (running watchOS 10+). Sorry to anyone on a Series 6 or earlier — Apple’s SDK doesn’t support older watches for new apps anymore.

Will it work without my phone nearby?

Yes — once installed, the app runs entirely on the watch. You don’t need cell signal, you don’t need your phone in range, you don’t need anything other than the watch on your wrist.

Can I share it with non-golfer friends?

Of course! It also works as a generic counter if you just want to tap a button a lot. (No judgment.)

What if I find a bug?

Tell me — hello@bytecraftco.ai or text. The fastest way to fix something is to know about it.

Is there an Android version?

Not yet. Apple Watch only for now. If enough people ask, I’ll think about Wear OS down the road.

Is this your real job?

No, this is a side project. ByteCraftCo is a tiny independent studio I run after hours. The day job pays the rent; Pin & Tally is for fun and for our league. Which is also why feedback matters so much — I want to spend my limited evening hours building stuff people actually like.
